## üìù Step-by-Step Learning Guide

### Week 1-2: A1 Level Basics

1. **Day 1-3**: Learn the German alphabet and pronunciation

   - Practice writing each letter
   - Learn special characters (√§, √∂, √º, √ü)
   - Practice pronunciation with audio

2. **Day 4-7**: Basic Greetings and Introductions

   - Learn common greetings
   - Practice introducing yourself
   - Learn to ask basic questions

3. **Day 8-14**: Numbers and Basic Vocabulary
   - Master numbers 1-20
   - Learn essential words for daily life
   - Practice simple conversations

### Week 3-4: A1 to A2 Transition

1. **Week 3**: Expand Your Vocabulary

   - Learn food and drink words
   - Practice ordering in restaurants
   - Learn family member names

2. **Week 4**: Basic Grammar
   - Learn present tense
   - Practice simple sentences
   - Learn question formation

### Week 5-8: A2 Level

1. **Week 5-6**: Daily Activities

   - Learn time expressions
   - Practice describing your day
   - Learn transportation vocabulary

2. **Week 7-8**: Shopping and Directions
   - Learn shopping vocabulary
   - Practice asking for directions
   - Learn to describe locations

### Week 9-12: B1 Level

1. **Week 9-10**: Past Tense

   - Learn past tense forms
   - Practice telling stories
   - Learn time expressions

2. **Week 11-12**: Complex Sentences
   - Learn conjunctions
   - Practice complex conversations
   - Learn to express opinions

### Week 13-16: B2 Level

1. **Week 13-14**: Professional German

   - Learn business vocabulary
   - Practice formal communication
   - Learn to write emails

2. **Week 15-16**: Advanced Topics
   - Learn complex grammar
   - Practice debates
   - Learn to express abstract ideas

## üí° Tips for Learning German

1. **Practice Daily**: Even 10 minutes a day helps
2. **Listen and Repeat**: Use the hover feature to practice pronunciation
3. **Learn in Context**: Words are easier to remember in sentences
4. **Start with Basics**: Master the alphabet and numbers first
5. **Use the Language**: Try to form simple sentences with the words you learn

## üéØ Next Steps

1. Practice the alphabet pronunciation
2. Learn to count from 1 to 20
3. Start using basic words in sentences
4. Build your vocabulary with more words
5. Practice speaking with native speakers

Remember: Learning a language takes time and practice. Be patient with yourself and enjoy the journey!
